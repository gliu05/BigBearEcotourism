About Us

Big Bear Ecotourism is a non-profit organization in coaliation with other local business vendors and local residents/community members. The purpose of this coalition is to support, encourage and promote Ecotourism in Big Bear. We plan and implement pathways that bring economic benefits to Big Bear Valley through the conservation of education about and promotion of our extraordinarily unique and diverse natural habitats, wildlife and resources.

What is Ecotourism? Ecotourism promotes travel to natural areas that conserves the environment and improves the well-being of local people. Ecotourism has a positive impact on both natural areas and the local community. Our principles of ecotourism include:

  • Minimize impact and build environmental and cultural awareness and respect.
  • Provide positive experiences for both visitors and hosts.
  • Provide direct financial benefits for conservation.
  • Provide financial benefits and empowerment for local people.
  • Raise sensitivity to host areaâ€™s political, environmental, and social climate.

When the snow melts away and water gets warmer, Big Bear Lake marinas open for the Spring through Fall seasons. Kayaks not only allow you to explore the shoreline and inlets where many motorized boats can't go, but also offer great exercise and fitness. Tour guide will be taking you around Pleasure Point Marina in 2 hour sessions.

  • Cost/Max party size: Free if you have your own kayak/supplies or else need to pay for rental fee from one of our partnered local vendors. Price of rentals subject so contact us. Max party size of 4.

Canoes can launch upon inspection and approval from public boat launch ramps or at marinas that serve along on the lake. Canoe guided tour schedule will be the same as kayaking schedule.

  • Cost/Max party size: Free if you have your own canoe/supplies or else need to pay for rental fee from one of our partnered local vendors. Price of rentals subject so contact us. Max party size of 4.

Offered mostly in the Spring, Summer, and Fall. Our guides will take you to some of the rarest habitats in the world such as Pebble Plains or Montane Meadows. These areas have wildlife and plants that you have never seen anywhere else and are only located exclusively here and nowhere else in the world!

  • Cost/Max party size: Free (donations graciously accepted). Max party size of 5.

Snowshoeing is a winter sport in which participants wear specially designed gear on their feet which distributes their weight, allowing them to walk on snow without breaking through it. Our tours are designed to give you an appreciation of our unique environment and an opportunity to spot wildlife while on tour. If there is something particular you would like to know, our guides are well versed in the natural history of the area and can answer most questions. Three hour sessions.

  • Cost/Max party size: Free if you have your own snowshoes, or else rental fee from one of your partnered local vendors. Contact us for rental pricing Max party size 4.
